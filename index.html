Top-down cut/build drawing — 3' - 4' - 3' split (recommended)

All dimensions in inches. R = circle radius the original bow was drawn on (≈47"). The two 3ft side shields are essentially flat (only ~0.9" of natural curve over their length — cut them straight). The 4ft center shield is bowed ~6" over its 48" length to keep the booth's full 81.35" depth; if you'd rather it be perfectly flat, depth shrinks to 75.36" at the center (toggle above shows both). Door is set into the left straight side wall, 30" wide.

Floor plan — plywood deck, joist grid & corner casters

Joist grid (≈16" o.c.) carries the plywood deck and holds the mineral-wool sound-deadening fill, same idea as your reference build. The floor (and the roof, directly above it) are both cut ~2.5" larger than the wall line all the way around, so they fully cover/cap the frame with a small lip rather than stopping flush at the studs. All 6 hexagon corners now get a locking caster (back-L, back-R, side-R, the 2 front panel joints, side-L) for full support and easy rolling.

Materials list (bill of materials)

Quantities are planning estimates sized to the dimensions/height above, with ~10–15% waste already factored into sheet/board counts. Round up to your supplier's stock sizes; always good to get 1 extra sheet of plywood for the inevitable mis-cut.
