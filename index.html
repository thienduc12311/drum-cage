Top-down cut/build drawing — 3' - 4' - 3' split (recommended)

All dimensions in inches. R = circle radius the original bow was drawn on (≈47"). All 3 front shields are cut flat — the two 3ft side shields are essentially flat anyway (only ~0.9" of natural curve over their length), and the 4ft center shield is also left flat rather than bowed, which brings the booth's depth at the center to 75.36" instead of the true 81.35" bow apex. Door is set into the left straight side wall, 30" wide.

Floor plan — plywood deck, joist grid & corner casters

Joist grid (≈16" o.c.) carries the plywood deck and holds the mineral-wool sound-deadening fill, same idea as your reference build. The floor is cut ~2.5" larger than the wall line all the way around, so it fully covers/caps the frame with a small lip rather than stopping flush at the studs (the top is now a light split plywood cap on 3 stick beams, not a heavy frame — see the framing/top sections below). All 6 hexagon corners now get a locking caster (back-L, back-R, side-R, the 2 front panel joints, side-L) for full support and easy rolling.

Detailed floor framing plan — every member dimensioned (deep/heavy, for mass & sound isolation)

This is the structural skeleton under the floor's plywood top deck and bottom skin — a torsion box. Use 2x6 (or deeper, to match the floor-thickness slider above) lumber on edge for the rim and every joist/runner shown, so the finished floor is the full 5–6"+ deep. The top of the cage is intentionally not built this heavy: it's a thin split plywood cap hanging on 3 light stick beams — see the dedicated "Top measurement plan" section below.

Back wall framing plan — every measurement, updates with the height slider

Standard stud-wall elevation for the 90"-wide back wall: a bottom plate, a top plate, and studs spaced evenly across the full width (6 equal bays, ≈15" each, close to standard 16" o.c. but with no leftover odd bay). This is the wall the plywood skin and any shelving/hardware screw into. The left side wall (48" wide) already divides evenly into 3 equal 16" bays; the right side wall (also 48") is now polycarbonate, so it only needs perimeter framing, not full studs.

Top measurement plan — split panel + 3-stick hanging frame

Top-down view of the lightweight cap: it's cut in half down the centerline (dashed gold line) into a left and right piece, rejoined with 3 splice plates across the seam. The 3 stick beams run side-to-side and rest their ends on the wall top plates (back rectangle gets 2 full-width beams, the front bow gets 1 shorter one) — the panel halves simply lay on top of this little ladder, nothing needs to stand up from the floor.

Materials list (bill of materials)
